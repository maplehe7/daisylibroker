Coto De Caza

Welcome to Coto de Caza

A rural and serene master-planned community

Coto de Caza is an affluent Census Designated Place in Orange County. Its luxurious estates have made a splash on the silver screen twice — first in 1984 when the community was the center for the Olympics’ pentathlon events, and again in the 21st century as the backdrop of the popular reality show, the Real Housewives of Orange County.

Like many neighborhoods in Orange County, Coto de Caza comes from humble beginnings. Originally a barley ranch, development in the area began in the middle of the 20th century. Coto de Caza was originally a private hunting reserve and equestrian club — the name Coto de Caza is Spanish for “hunting preserve.”

With a population of 14,886, Coto de Caza has a close-knit sense of community. Residents enjoy the serenity and seclusion of the region — it’s easy to feel far from the hustle and bustle of urban life among Coto de Caza’s oak and sycamore groves.

Ever since its days as a hunting preserve, Coto de Caza has maintained a reputation of exclusivity and luxury. It has fewer than 5,000 homes, all within its gates, so real estate is in high demand.

There are many different architectural styles in Coto de Caza, but especially Spanish Colonial, Meditteranean, and Tuscan. The lots are known for being large and sprawling estates, and many have space for stables. The median home price in Coto de Caza is around $2.9 million.

What to Love

  • Secluded and exclusive gated community
  • Plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure — whether hiking, biking, or on horseback
  • Beautiful views of the Santa Ana Mountains
  • Large, estate-like homes on spacious lots

People & Lifestyle

Coto de Caza is a small community that feels comfortably tucked away from the hustle and bustle of Southern California — it is quite literally gated off. Because of this, it is a serene, tight-knit community. Don’t expect wild nightlife and busy streets; Coto de Caza brings rural charm to Orange County. Due to its exclusivity, Coto de Caza also has a reputation for glamor, luxury, and affluence.

Dining, Shopping, & Entertainment

Preserve Restaurant, Craftsman Restaurant, and El Molino are all located in the Coto de Caza Golf & Racquet Club. Preserve is an upscale dining destination, Craftsman is a casual lounge and bar, and El Molino focuses on tapas and other sharable plates and entrees.

For more Latin-inspired cuisine, head to Tapas & Beer. The restaurant serves “Nuevo Latino” dishes, which means that they’re inspired by a multitude of Latin cultures, including Spanish, Cuban, and Caribbean. The restaurant also boasts a full bar with 13 beers on tap.

Hanna’s is a sophisticated steakhouse serving New American fare — it was even named one of the top 100 restaurants in America by OpenTable. For a more casual setting, The Stand offers fast-casual American eats like cheeseburgers, hot dogs, sandwiches, and craft beers.

Il Sole Cucina is an upscale Italian restaurant featuring authentic pasta dishes, including goat cheese and fig ravioli and authentic Italian-style pizza.

Things to Do

Residents of Coto de Caza aren’t just surrounded by the community’s gates, but they’re also encompassed by the gorgeous sycamore and oak groves in the Thomas F. Riley Wilderness Park, which has over 6 miles of hiking, biking, and equestrian trails to explore. The Wilderness Park also has a butterfly garden and is a favorite spot for local photographers.

Golfers will enjoy the Coto De Caza Golf & Racquet Club, which has 36 holes of golf — including the North Course, which is ranked in the nation’s top 25 courses. It also has a large craftsman-style clubhouse, a racquet club with ten courts, an aquatic center, a sports club, and a spa.

The Coto Valley Country Club has a well-manicured grand lawn, pastures, an aquatic center, and a fitness center. Visitors can also enjoy the non-member events that the country club hosts all throughout the year.

Residents can participate in Coto de Caza’s rural culture at one of the many equestrian centers in the community — whether they are experienced riders or brand new to the sport. The Coto Equestrian Preserve is a 24 acre complex with six riding arenas, easy access to Coto de Caza’s equestrian trails, and around 150 horses boarded in the stables. It was also the location for the equestrian pentathlon events during the 1984 Olympics.

Schools

Coto de Caza is served by the Capistrano Unified School District.
