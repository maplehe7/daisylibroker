Newport Beach

Welcome to Newport Beach

An affluent, seaside city with a thriving harbor

Newport Beach is an affluent and bayside community in Orange County. The city is home to the largest recreational harbor on the West Coast, so there’s plenty to do in and around the sea.

Long thought to be too dangerous for ships to pass, the Newport Bay remained virtually unexplored until the end of the 19th century. In 1870, the first ship successfully docked in the new port — hence the name Newport.

Newport Beach soon became a bustling community, and it was incorporated as a city in 1906. The growth was fueled by McFadden Wharf, a bustling wharf built in 1888 that was the largest business in Orange County at the time. Nowadays, Newport Pier stands where McFadden Wharf once stood.

Due to its maritime roots, Newport Beach is a popular spot for sailors of all kinds — whether their vessel of choice is a humble fishing craft or a sleek and stylish sailboat.

While land in the area went for $1 an acre in the late 19th century, the city is now affluent and desirable, with a typical home price is around $2.6 million. The population of Newport Beach is 85,694.

What to Love

  • Bustling, seaside harbor with plenty of water-based activities
  • Ocean views and temperate weather all across the city
  • Renowned cafés, restaurants, bars, and shopping districts

People & Lifestyle

Newport Beach is a versatile and laid-back waterfront city. It has a lively, urban community along the bay and the coastline that is perfect for residents seeking the excitement of the town and peaceful suburbs for those looking for a quieter place to put down roots.

Dining, Shopping, & Entertainment

The Beachcomber is a coastal staple located in a converted cottage on the beach. A patio leads right to the sand, and patrons can rent beach chairs and fire pits from the café itself. The Beachcomber is also home to the Bootlegger Bar, where you can enjoy cocktails while watching the sunset over the Pacific.

For New American cuisine, head to the Farmhouse at Roger Gardens, which serves upscale, farm-to-fork dishes in a garden-like restaurant. A Restaurant located in the Mariner’s Mile is a New American restaurant with classic red leather booths and a full bar. Right next door is the aptly named A Market, which serves baked goods, beverages, and wine, as well as breakfast and lunch.

For more upscale American cuisine, pay a visit to the Pelican Grill, a hilltop restaurant with sweeping views of the sea. If you’re in the mood for Italian instead, Andrea is an Italian restaurant right next door, also boasting ocean views.

Marche Moderne is a chic French restaurant, Mayur serves upscale Indian food and champagne brunch, and Javier’s is a stylish Mexican restaurant with a well-stocked tequila selection.

Newport Beach is home to various seafood restaurants that serve fresh fish daily, including Eddie V’s Prime Seafood, Gulfstream, and Rusty Pelican, which has live music every Friday and Saturday. The Bluewater Grill is a bayside seafood restaurant and bar with its very own dock, so you can skip the traffic and take the boat to dinner instead.

For those who’d rather cook than dine out, the Historic Dory Fleet Market, located at the Newport Pier, has been connecting anglers with shoppers since 1891.

For sweet treats, head out to Balboa Island and stop by the local landmark Sugar N’ Spice. It has been in business since 1945 and sells frozen bananas and Balboa Bars — a local ice cream favorite.

Newport Beach is home to several shopping centers, including Fashion Island and Lido Marina Village — both have shops, restaurants, and cafés.

Things to Do

Crystal Cove State Park is a protected stretch of the Orange County Coastline. It includes over three acres of beach, as well as 2,400 acres of wilderness, and even a 1930s seaside cottage village that is home to the Beachcomber Cafe and rental cottages.

There are several beaches to choose from, including The Wedge (a popular spot for local surfers) and Corona Del Mar State Beach. Inspiration Point is the perfect place to watch the sunset.

Newport Beach Pier, built on the original site of the McFadden Wharf, is the perfect spot to go for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing. The pier also has an extensive bike trail, perfect for a ride, run, or walk along the sand. Following the trail takes you right to Balboa Pier, less than two miles away.

Balboa Island is located within the bay and is accessible by bridge, boat, and ferry. This community is charming with a lot to explore, and the island is ringed by pedestrian paths so visitors and residents can stroll along the ocean.

Sherman Library and Gardens is a haven for history-lovers and gardeners alike. It features a 2.2-acre botanical garden as well as a library with artifacts and historical documents from multiple periods of Southern California history.

Newport Sailing Adventures offers ocean excursions, sunset cruises, and whale watching on a fleet of varied ships so that you can sail the seas in style. For a taste of Venice in Southern California, Gondola Adventures offers romantic gondola rides and waterfront dinners.

Schools

Newport Beach is served by the Newport-Mesa Unified School District.
