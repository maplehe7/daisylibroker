Lake Forest

Welcome to Lake Forest

A tight-knit community surrounded by eucalyptus groves

Nestled within a eucalyptus grove and just a short drive from the sea, Lake Forest is a small and serene town in Orange County. It is situated southeast of the larger city of Irvine.

Originally ranchland, like much of the region, Lake Forest was home to a small community called El Toro. At the turn of the 20th century, a resident of El Toro named Dwight Whiting planted 400 acres of eucalyptus trees, creating the forest that gives Lake Forest its name.

In the middle of the 20th century, residential development began within the eucalyptus forest, and the quiet agricultural town of El Toro started to grow. In 1991, the community was officially incorporated as a city, and the name was changed from El Toro to Lake Forest to pay homage to the area’s natural beauty.

Like its neighboring Orange County cities, Lake Forest was a master-planned community first developed in the mid-century. Because of this, it is well-designed and boasts a variety of homes, including single-family homes, luxury estates, condominiums, duplexes, and apartments.

Lake Forest has a range of architectural styles, including the mid-century classic California Modern style and the newer Meditteranean style found in the Baker Ranch neighborhood. The median price of a home in Lake Forest is approximately $870,000, and the city’s population is around 87,000.

What to Love

  • Quiet community within commuting distance to the larger city of Irvine
  • Natural beauty, including eucalyptus groves and small lakes, as well as views of the Saddleback Mountains
  • The thriving and diverse restaurant scene

People & Lifestyle

Lake Forest is a relatively quiet city full of parks, small lakes, and beautiful scenery. Because of this, it has a serene quality that appeals to its residents. It is largely suburban, with a low crime rate, and is within easy commuting distance to other urban centers, making it perfect for professionals.

Dining, Shopping, & Entertainment

Fans of Asian cuisine have plenty of choices in Lake Forest — including Fam’s Kitchen, an Asian fusion restaurant, and Umma’s K-BBQ, a Korean barbecue joint. Phuud specializes in Vietnamese comfort food, and O’Shine Taiwanese Kitchen offers authentic Taiwanese cuisine.

For hearty dishes as well as a great happy hour menu, head to Brü Grill & Market. This restaurant has a contemporary casual vibe as well as fresh farm-to-fork dishes.

Ironwood offers upscale American dining, complete with a full bar and views of the Saddleback Mountains. For early meals, Rose Cafe is a fast-casual café that serves large portions and typical brunch fare. They offer meals like lox bagels, breakfast burritos, and a variety of different sandwiches.

Renzo’s is a taste of Peru, right in Lake Forest. Owned by Peruvian chef Renzo Machiavelli, Renzo’s serves authentic cuisine from Machiavello’s homeland, including ceviche, saltado, and more.

For locally crafted wine, pay a visit to Frisby Cellars. It was founded by a former firefighter and paramedic, Josh Frisby, an Orange County local with a passion for winemaking. Frisby Cellars’ wine is aged and blended right in Lake Forest, and the tasting room is open on the weekends.

Things to Do

Heritage Hill Historical Park is a popular park with four restored historic buildings that help tell Lake Forest’s history — from the days of the Mexican ranchos in the mid 19th century to the burgeoning beginnings of the El Toro community. Guided tours of the historic buildings are available, and visitors can also walk around the park’s four acres to take in the fresh air.

For more ways to experience local history, head down to Modjeska Canyon to visit the Arden: Helena Modjeska Historic House and Gardens. Helena Modjeska was a world-renowned Shakespearean actress from Poland in the late 19th century who moved to the brand new community of Orange County with her husband in 1876. She learned English and took the American stage by storm, debuting her U.S. career in San Francisco before touring the country.

Madame Modjeska, as she was called, was a fascinating Orange County pioneer. She called her home the Arden House, and the woods around it the Forest of Arden, named after the famous enchanted forest in Shakespeare’s “As You Like It.” Her home is open to the public for guided tours, where visitors can learn about her fascinating life and career.

Speaking of Madame Modjeska, Lake Forest residents can enjoy a performance at the local theater named in her honor, the Modjeska Playhouse.

Schools

Lake Forest is served by the Saddleback Valley Unified School District.
